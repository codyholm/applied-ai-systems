Indie pop sits between the polished hooks of mainstream pop and the looser textures of rock and acoustic music — jangly guitars, lighter drum production, and vocals that lean conversational rather than belted. Tempos cluster between 85 and 130 BPM, broad enough to support both dreamy mid-tempo cuts and brighter dancefloor-leaning singles. Acoustic content runs higher than mainstream pop — typically 0.25 to 0.6 — because real guitars and pianos commonly sit at the front of the mix. The genre's mood palette is wide: happy and upbeat on its sunnier cuts, melancholy when the production thins out and the lyrics turn introspective.
