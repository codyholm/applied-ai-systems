Lofi typically sits between 60 and 90 BPM and leans heavily on warm, vinyl-style filters — tape hiss, soft saturation, and rolled-off high frequencies that mimic worn cassette and dusty wax. Beats are deliberately laid-back, often built from dusty drum samples paired with looped piano, Rhodes, or muted guitar. Acoustic content runs high — usually above 0.6 — because the genre prizes unprocessed-sounding instrumentation over synthetic textures. Lofi pairs most naturally with focused, chill, and relaxed moods, and is the canonical "study with headphones on" genre.
