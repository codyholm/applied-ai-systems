Jazz centres on improvisation, swung rhythm, and acoustic instrumentation — upright bass, piano, brass, and brushed drums are its signature voices. Tempos vary widely with subgenre: smooth and cool jazz live in the 70s–90s BPM range, while bebop and hard bop push past 120 BPM. Acoustic content is consistently high because amplification is light and the recorded instruments are largely unprocessed. The genre pairs most naturally with relaxed and chill moods in its quieter forms, and tilts toward moody and intense in its more dissonant or up-tempo cuts.
