Hip hop is built on programmed beats, sampled or synthesised low end, and vocal performance — rap, half-sung delivery, or rhythmic spoken word — sitting on top of the groove. Tempos most commonly land between 80 and 110 BPM, with a deliberate behind-the-beat feel that supports head-nod rhythm rather than dancefloor energy. Danceability values are consistently high because the genre is fundamentally rhythm-led, even on slower cuts. Hip hop pairs broadly across moods — chill on lo-fi-leaning instrumentals, focused on driving boom-bap, and intense when the production turns toward maximalist trap or hard-hitting breaks.
