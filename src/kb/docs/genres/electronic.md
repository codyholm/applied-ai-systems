Electronic is the broad umbrella for music produced primarily on synthesisers, drum machines, and computers — house, techno, downtempo, IDM, and adjacent styles. Tempos most commonly sit between 100 and 130 BPM where danceable four-on-the-floor patterns dominate, though downtempo cuts can drop into the 80s. Acoustic content stays low because the spectrum is dominated by synthesised waveforms and processed samples rather than recorded acoustic sources. Mood coverage is wide: upbeat on club-leaning cuts, focused on driving instrumental grooves, and moody on washed-out, atmospheric productions.
