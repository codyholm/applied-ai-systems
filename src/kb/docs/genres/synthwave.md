Synthwave draws directly from the analogue-synth palette of 1980s film scores and arcade soundtracks — gated reverb on drums, saw-wave leads, glassy chorused pads, and pulsing arpeggios. Tempos cluster between 100 and 130 BPM, supporting steady, danceable grooves rather than complex rhythmic shifts. Acoustic content is uniformly low because the genre is essentially all-electronic by design. The mood palette skews moody and atmospheric on slower cuts and tilts toward upbeat or intense when the synth arpeggios speed up and layer with driving drums.
