Melancholy combines low energy with low valence — the music is quiet and the affect is sad rather than neutral. Energy values typically sit between 0.15 and 0.50, and valence stays in the 0.15 to 0.45 band where minor keys, slow tempos, and unresolved harmonic motion dominate. Common genre pairings are acoustic when the focus is on a single voice and instrument, indie pop on its sparse introspective cuts, and jazz on its more wistful late-night repertoire. Melancholy is the listening mode for processing rather than escape — letters to no one, long evenings, looking through old photos.
