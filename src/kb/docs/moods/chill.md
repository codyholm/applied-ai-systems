Chill describes a low-energy, mid-valence listening state — the music is unhurried but not sad, present but not demanding. Energy values typically sit between 0.20 and 0.55, and valence stays in a neutral-to-mildly-positive 0.4 to 0.75 band. Common genre pairings are lofi, ambient, acoustic, and jazz — styles that prize slow tempos, warm timbres, and minimal rhythmic intensity. Chill is the standard request for late afternoons, low-stakes work sessions, and unwinding without checking out completely.
