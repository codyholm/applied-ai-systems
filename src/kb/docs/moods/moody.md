Moody music carries a darker emotional cast without crossing into outright melancholy — minor-key harmonies, longer reverbs, and more deliberate, weighted rhythm. Valence values typically sit between 0.25 and 0.55, dragging the affect lower while energy stays moderate to high. Common genre pairings are synthwave on its slower atmospheric cuts, rock on mid-tempo brooders, jazz on its more dissonant late-night repertoire, and electronic on washed-out, downtempo productions. Moody fits night drives, rainy-window listening, and contexts where the listener wants atmosphere with bite rather than comfort.
